## 5. Objection Handling

### Objection: "Spreadsheets are free"

**Response framework:**

1. **Acknowledge:** "Totally fair. Spreadsheets work‚Äîfor about two weeks."
2. **Reframe:** "The question isn't cost. It's: will you actually use it?"
3. **Evidence:** "90% of job search spreadsheets are abandoned within 14 days. No reminders. No accountability. No system."
4. **Close:** "CoreLine costs less than one networking coffee per month. And it actually gets used."

**Quick-hit versions:**
- "Spreadsheets don't ping you when it's time to follow up."
- "Free doesn't help when you forget to open it."
- "Your career is worth $14.95/month."

---

### Objection: "I'm not actively job hunting"

**Response:**
"Perfect. Neither are most of our users. CoreLine is career insurance‚Äîit keeps your network warm so you're never caught off-guard. Five minutes a week now saves five months of scrambling later."

---

### Objection: "I already use LinkedIn"

**Response:**
"LinkedIn is where connections live. CoreLine is where relationships get nurtured. When's the last time LinkedIn reminded you to check in with a former colleague? To follow up with a recruiter? We're the layer that makes LinkedIn actually work."

---

### Objection: "I'll sign up when I need it"

**Response:**
"That's like buying fire insurance after the fire. The whole point is having a warm network BEFORE you need it. The best time to start was a year ago. The second best time is today."

## 7. Launch Offer Recommendation

### Recommended: **Founding Member Pricing**

**Structure:**
- **$9.95/month** (locked for life) for first 100 subscribers
- Regular price: $14.95/month
- Creates urgency + early adopter loyalty

**Why this works:**
- ‚úÖ Creates scarcity (100 spots)
- ‚úÖ Rewards early believers
- ‚úÖ Doesn't devalue the product (it's a reward, not desperation)
- ‚úÖ Locks in long-term users at lower CAC
- ‚úÖ "Founding member" language builds community

### Alternative Options Considered:

| Offer | Pros | Cons |
|-------|------|------|
| **Free trial (14-day)** | Low barrier | Credit card friction, high churn |
| **Freemium** | Growth | Complicates monetization, delays revenue |
| **Discount (30% off)** | Clear value | Feels transactional, not special |
| **Annual discount** | Better LTV | Too much commitment early |

### Launch Sequence:
1. **Week 1:** Waitlist with "Founding Member" positioning
2. **Week 2:** Open to waitlist (first 100 get locked pricing)
3. **Week 3:** Public launch at full price
4. **Week 4:** Consider limited "last chance" for stragglers

## 8. KPIs to Track Success

### North Star Metric
**Active Relationship Managers (ARMs):** Users who log in and take action 2+ times per week

*Why:* This measures real engagement, not vanity signups.

---

### Launch Phase KPIs (Days 1-30)

| Category | Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|----------|--------|--------|----------------|
| **Acquisition** | Waitlist signups | 500 | Validates demand |
| **Acquisition** | Paid conversions | 100 | Revenue validation |
| **Acquisition** | CAC | <$25 | Sustainable growth |
| **Activation** | Users who add 5+ contacts | 70% | First value moment |
| **Activation** | Day 1 retention | >50% | Product stickiness |
| **Engagement** | Weekly active users | 60% | Habit formation |
| **Revenue** | MRR | $1,000-$1,500 | Business validation |
| **Retention** | 30-day retention | >40% | Product-market fit signal |
